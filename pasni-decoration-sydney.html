Pasni decoration in Sydney, styled with cultural care

Pasni is one of the first major milestones in a Nepalese baby's life, and the decoration should feel warm, respectful, and photo-ready without overwhelming the family ceremony. Khusi Events & Decor styles pasni celebrations across Sydney for Nepalese families who want cultural detail, soft colours, and a calm setup process. We can prepare the backdrop, pasni seating area, plinths, cake table, welcome board, florals, signage, candles, and selected traditional styling elements. Every package includes full on-site setup and pack-down, with delivery quoted separately by location. Families can choose a ready package or customise colours, garlands, signage text, and display pieces for the home, hall, or venue.

FAQ

Pasni decoration FAQs

When is pasni usually celebrated?

Pasni is commonly celebrated when a baby is around five to six months old, depending on family tradition and priest guidance.

Do you manage the ceremony order?

We style the event space and decor. Families usually manage the ceremony order, priest, food, and ritual items, but we can design the setup around those needs.

What do families provide and what does Khusi supply?

Khusi supplies the agreed decor pieces, backdrop, florals, signage, plinths, setup, and pack-down. Families usually provide ritual items, clothing, food, and personal ceremony pieces.
